I believe that VR can do something much deeper than just entertain.

It can physically displace, mentally confound. It can create not just mindless joy, but deep discomfort. Not the kind that you feel when you see a creepy zombie, but rather the kind when you meet your truest form, when you have to fully consider what it means to be present with yourself, alone, in completely alien world.

Creating these sorts of progressive experiences takes time, energy, and freedom. If I don't have to spend this time and energy on trying to find ways to make money, I can focus on what the medium needs most:

radical experimentation.

Because of this, I am looking to find funding to fully cover my research in 2017. Enough to allow myself the luxury of experimentation. To try and find the parts of VR that are beyond entertainment.

Discover parts of our reality that were until now relegated to the ether.

- Release a mutually agreed upon number of experiments
